No Conversion Educational Framework

Global Peace Through Religious Literacy – The "No Proselytization" Approach

Created by Kallol Chakrabarti
Independent Researcher

Overview

The No Conversion Educational Framework is a 100% original global initiative designed to foster lasting peace by promoting religious literacy without any form of proselytization or conversion narratives.

It addresses the root belief that conversion-driven narratives are a primary driver of religious intolerance, persecution, and minority atrocities. By replacing narrative-based conflict with neutral, evidence-based education, this framework aims to raise informed, empathetic citizens and break cycles of religious conflict.

Core Principles

  • Strict non-proselytization mandate – education only, no persuasion or ranking of faiths
  • Evidence-based content drawn from peer-reviewed scholarship and primary religious texts
  • Localized implementation and adaptation by regional interfaith councils
  • Delivery by certified multi-faith educator teams

Key Features

  • Modular curriculum addressing myths, misconceptions, shared values, and historical conflict points
  • Multi-format delivery: digital platforms, case studies, role-playing, workshops, and school integration
  • Regional customization (e.g., India: Hinduism modules on caste & polytheism; Middle East: Judaism/Islam; West: extremism & women's rights)
  • Scalable implementation tiers: self-learning → community dialogues → institutional train-the-trainer
  • Interfaith governance and measurable outcomes (empathy metrics, reduction in inter-religious incidents)
  • Sustainable funding model combining grants, CSR, government support, and micro-certifications

Vision & Call to Action
